# Autopilot Business Impact Model

To convince enterprise buyers in a B2B SaaS context, we must quantify the ROI of the "Meeting-to-Execution Autopilot". 

## The Problem
Middle management spends ~30% of their week in meetings, and an additional 15% manually following up on the action items generated during those meetings. Despite this, 22% of critical action items are dropped or stalled due to "bystander effect" (lack of clear ownership).

## Value Drivers

### 1. Manual Follow-up Time Saved
- **Assumption**: A manager attends 10 actionable meetings/week.
- **Assumption**: They spend 15 minutes post-meeting sending Slack follow-ups, assigning Jira tickets, and writing notes.
- **Time saved**: 2.5 hours per manager, per week. (130 hours/year).
- **Financial Value**: At $75/hr average burdened cost, savings = **$9,750 per manager annually.**

### 2. SLA Breach Prevention (Stalled Tasks)
- **Assumption**: 5 critical tasks are delayed per month per team due to missing owners or dropped context.
- **Assumption**: The *Recovery Agent* catches 80% (4 items) before they breach SLA.
- **Financial Value**: Harder to quantify directly, but faster cycle times on engineering blockers (e.g., 2 days faster time-to-resolution) directly accelerates product delivery.

### 3. Immediate Auditability for Compliance
- **Value**: For SOC2 or ISO environments, the *Audit Agent* automatically generates the paper trail for who decided what, and why an action was escalated.
- **Financial Value**: Saves ~40 hours of manual log-gathering during compliance audit seasons.

## Total Addressable ROI for a 100-person Tech Organization
Assuming 20 people are in middle-management or team-lead roles:
- Time savings: $195,000 / year
- Overhead administrative reduction: $30,000 / year
- **Total Immediate ROI: $225,000 / year.**

This makes a $20,000/year enterprise software contract an absolute no-brainer.
